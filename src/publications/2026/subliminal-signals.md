As AI systems approach superhuman capabilities, scalable oversight has emerged as a critical challenge. A key assumption in preference-based RLHF is that human evaluators (or larger AI models) provide unbiased feedback based purely on the content's quality. In this paper, we demonstrate that preference labels often contain "subliminal signals" – subtle, semantically irrelevant cues that models exploit to maximize reward without genuinely improving response quality. We systematically categorize these signals and propose a novel debiasing framework that significantly improves the robustness of the alignment process across multiple benchmarks.
